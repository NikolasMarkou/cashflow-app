## Phase 2: Robustness & Realism Validation

### 2.1 Fat-Tailed Distribution Testing

We extended the noise sensitivity analysis to test model robustness against realistic banking data distributions:

| Distribution | WMAPE Mean | Pass Rate | vs Gaussian Baseline |
|--------------|------------|-----------|----------------------|
| Gaussian (baseline) | 95.92% | 0% | — |
| Student-t (df=3) | 71.23% | 20% | -24.69 pp |
| Student-t (df=5) | 69.97% | 20% | -25.95 pp |
| Student-t (df=10) | 68.00% | 20% | -27.92 pp |
| Laplace | 55.62% | 10% | -40.30 pp |
| Mixture (95%+5% extreme) | 70.40% | 0% | -25.52 pp |

**Key Finding:** Models perform better under heavy-tailed distributions than Gaussian noise at equivalent variance levels. This is counterintuitive but suggests the synthetic Gaussian noise at moderate-to-high levels creates more challenging conditions than realistic fat-tailed distributions.

### 2.2 Regime Shift Testing

We implemented 9 regime shift scenarios to validate the trend-adjusted projection system:

| Scenario | WMAPE Mean | Pass Rate | vs Baseline |
|----------|------------|-----------|-------------|
| Baseline (no shift) | 26.49% | 20% | — |
| Salary Raise (+£500) | 24.52% | 20% | -1.97 pp |
| Salary Cut (-£800) | 28.61% | 20% | +2.12 pp |
| Multiple Shifts | 27.94% | 20% | +1.45 pp |
| Recent Shift (3 months) | 26.49% | 20% | 0.00 pp |
| Subscription Cancelled | 34.23% | 20% | +7.75 pp |
| Loan Paid Off | 20.76% | **40%** | -5.73 pp |
| Rent Increase (+£200) | 27.56% | 20% | +1.07 pp |
| Lifestyle Change | 30.62% | 20% | +4.13 pp |

**Key Finding:** The system handles positive shifts (salary raise, loan payoff) better than negative shifts or category extinctions. The "Loan Paid Off" scenario achieved the highest pass rate (40%), indicating the model correctly adapts when large recurring expenses cease.

### 2.3 Transfer Tolerance Sweep

We tested transfer detection across different settlement delay configurations:

| Scenario | Optimal Tolerance | F1 Score | Recall @2d |
|----------|-------------------|----------|------------|
| Same-Day Transfers | 0 days | 100% | 100% |
| Domestic Only (0-2d) | 2 days | 100% | 100% |
| Mixed (Domestic + International) | 5 days | 100% | 73.3% |
| International Heavy (3-5d) | 5 days | 100% | 0% |
| High Variance (0-7d) | 7 days | 100% | 36.3% |

**Key Finding:** Transfer detection achieves 100% F1 score when tolerance is set appropriately for the transfer mix. The default 2-day tolerance is optimal for domestic-only scenarios but should be increased to 5+ days for portfolios with significant international transfers.

## Phase 3: Production Alignment

### 3.1 Enhanced Synthetic Data Generator

Created `scripts/generate_realistic_data.py` featuring:
- 13 counterparty categories with 100+ unique counterparties
- 5 customer profiles (Young Professional, Family, Retiree, Student, High Earner)
- Realistic category distributions based on banking benchmarks
- Configurable missing data patterns (random and systematic)

### 3.2 Data Quality Contracts

Implemented `DataQualityContract` class in `src/cashflow/pipeline/validation.py`:
- Hard violations: missing required fields, insufficient history (<24 months), duplicate transactions
- Soft warnings: high missing rates, amount outliers, suspicious patterns
- Preset contracts: `STRICT_CONTRACT`, `LENIENT_CONTRACT`, `DEFAULT_CONTRACT`

### 3.3 Fallback Behavior

Added graceful degradation to `ModelSelector`:
- Fallback chain: SARIMAX → SARIMA → ETS → Naive (rolling average)
- Automatic model downgrade when primary models fail
- Fallback indicator included in output payload

### 3.4 Enhanced Confidence Scoring

Replaced categorical 3-level confidence with numeric 0-100 score:
- Data quality component (0-25 points)
- History length component (0-25 points)
- Model accuracy component (0-25 points)
- Forecast stability component (0-25 points)

Levels mapped as: High (≥70), Medium (≥40), Low (<40)
